# Club Cricket — Frequently Asked Questions (Members' Handbook v3)

### How many players are on a team?
Each side fields eleven players. For our Sunday social league, however, we play
a modified format with eight players per side to make it easier to field a team.
Note this differs from the standard format described elsewhere in this handbook.

### How long is a match?
It depends on the format. Our weekday evening games are 10 overs per side. Our
Saturday league games are 40 overs per side. The youth section plays 20 overs.
Test-style multi-day games are not played at this club.

### What is the powerplay?
In our 40-over league, the first 9 overs are a powerplay during which only two
fielders are allowed outside the 30-yard circle. In the 10-over evening format
there is no powerplay at all. (Different leagues set different powerplay rules,
so always check the specific competition regulations.)

### Can I bowl as many overs as I want?
No. In the 40-over format, no bowler may bowl more than 8 overs. In the 10-over
format, no bowler may bowl more than 2 overs. These limits are stricter than the
international ODI limit of 10 overs per bowler.

### What should I bring to a match?
Whites for league games, coloured kit for evening games. Bring your own bat if you
have one; the club has shared kit otherwise. Spiked shoes are recommended but
metal spikes are banned on the artificial practice pitches.

### Who pays for the match tea?
The home team is responsible for providing the match tea. Costs are reimbursed
from club funds up to a limit of fifteen pounds per match. The away team covers
its own travel.

### What happens if it rains?
The umpires decide if play is possible. For league games, a result can be declared
if both sides have faced at least 20 overs. For evening games, at least 5 overs per
side are required for a result. Otherwise the match is abandoned and points shared.

### How do I report a score?
Scores must be submitted through the club app within 24 hours of the match. The
team captain is responsible for submission. Late submissions may incur a one-point
deduction in the league standings.
