# Cricket — A Quick Reference

## The basics
Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players each.
One team bats, trying to score runs, while the other team bowls and fields,
trying to restrict runs and dismiss the batters. The roles then switch.

## Scoring runs
Batters score runs by hitting the ball and running between the two sets of stumps,
exchanging ends. A ball that reaches the boundary scores four runs if it touches
the ground on the way, and six runs if it clears the boundary on the full.

## Getting out
There are several ways a batter can be dismissed. The most common are: bowled
(the ball hits the stumps), caught (a fielder catches the ball before it bounces),
leg before wicket (the ball would have hit the stumps but struck the batter's leg
first), run out (the stumps are broken while batters are running), and stumped
(the wicket-keeper breaks the stumps while the batter is out of the crease).

## Formats
The three main formats differ mainly in length. Test cricket is played over up to
five days with two innings per side. One Day Internationals limit each side to a
single innings of 50 overs. Twenty20 is the shortest format, with each side facing
just 20 overs, which makes it fast and high-scoring.

## The over
An over consists of six legal deliveries bowled consecutively by one bowler from
one end. After an over is completed, a different bowler bowls the next over from
the opposite end.
